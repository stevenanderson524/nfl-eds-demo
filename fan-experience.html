Iconic NFL Stadiums

Caesars Superdome

Caesars Superdome — New Orleans, Louisiana

Home of the New Orleans Saints and host of Super Bowl LIX (February 2026), the Superdome is one of the most iconic venues in American sports history.

Capacity: 73,208 | Opened: 1975 | Super Bowl LIX Host

New Orleans Saints

SoFi Stadium

SoFi Stadium — Inglewood, California

One of the most technologically advanced stadiums in the world, SoFi Stadium features a 70,240-seat bowl, a massive translucent roof, and a breathtaking double-sided scoreboard.

Capacity: 70,240 | Opened: 2020 | Host: LA Rams & LA Chargers

Los Angeles Rams · Los Angeles Chargers

Allegiant Stadium

Allegiant Stadium — Las Vegas, Nevada

The dazzling home of the Las Vegas Raiders features a retractable natural grass field, stunning black glass exterior, and a 65,000-seat capacity perfect for the entertainment capital of the world.

Capacity: 65,000 | Opened: 2020 | Host: Las Vegas Raiders

Las Vegas Raiders

Arrowhead Stadium

Arrowhead Stadium — Kansas City, Missouri

One of the loudest stadiums in the world, Arrowhead has set the Guinness World Record for crowd noise. The home of the Kansas City Chiefs is electric on every gameday.

Capacity: 76,416 | Opened: 1972 | Host: Kansas City Chiefs

Kansas City Chiefs
