Campaign Layout: It Takes All of Us

NFL Blue Background Treatment

NFL Shield Blue (#003369) as the full-bleed background. All-Pro Display at maximum scale fills the frame. White type only — no color headline accents on this background.

Shield placement: Upper left or upper right, 10–15% of layout width. Always reversed to white on blue backgrounds.

Typography: All-Pro Display A, all caps, stacked vertically for maximum impact. Leading set tight — 90% of cap height.

Campaign Layout: All Paths Lead Here

Photo Collage Treatment

Multiple photographs assembled in a grid or collage format. Bold headline overlaid or placed adjacent. Shows the diversity of NFL storylines — players, fans, teams, communities.

Grid structure: Maintain consistent gutter widths. Images should vary in size to create visual hierarchy — lead image at 2× the area of supporting images.

Typography: All-Pro Display, either reversed white on dark photo areas or set in NFL Shield Blue on white/light backgrounds adjacent to the collage.

Layout Principles

Every NFL layout is built on three elements working together: the Shield, typography, and imagery. No layout is complete without all three in proper proportion and placement.

Shield Rules

The Shield must always have clear space equal to the width of the "N" in NFL around all sides. Never distort, rotate, or add effects to the Shield.

Typography Scale

Headlines should dominate. Body copy is secondary and should never compete with the headline for visual weight. Use Endzone Sans or Slab for body — never All-Pro Display at small sizes.

Color in Layouts

NFL Shield Blue is the default background. NFL Shield Red is an accent, not a background color. Never place NFL Shield Red text on NFL Shield Blue without sufficient contrast testing.
