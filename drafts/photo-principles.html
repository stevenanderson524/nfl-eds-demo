NFL Visual Identity

Photography Principles

NFL photography captures emotion, intensity, and the human stories at the heart of the game. Three distinct styles work together to tell the full story of football.

Dramatic Cropping

Bold, editorial crops that isolate raw emotion and physical intensity. B&W treatment for maximum dramatic effect.

Locker Room Intensity

Tight crops isolate the moment. B&W removes distraction and forces focus on the subject's intensity and emotion.

Key technique: Crop to obscure jersey numbers and team identifiers when representing the NFL broadly — the athlete's emotion is the story, not the team affiliation.

Game Face

Close-in portraiture that reveals the psychological intensity of preparation and competition. Eye black, focused gaze, raw determination.

Key technique: High contrast B&W processing. Leave breathing room at the crop edges — the subject should feel contained, not cramped.

Fan & Community

Authentic moments of celebration, connection, and shared experience. Color photography that captures the joy and energy of NFL fandom.

Fan photography should feel real — not staged. Capture genuine emotional reactions, multi-generational moments, and the diversity of the NFL fanbase.

Lambeau Leap — Fan Celebration

Iconic moments of player-fan connection. Color photography to capture the energy and joy. Wide enough to show context — the crowd, the venue, the shared moment.

Youth & Community

The next generation of NFL fans. Authentic, warm, and optimistic. These images show the NFL's role in communities — not just stadiums, but neighborhoods, schools, and families.

Team Energy

The collective power of a team — celebration, sideline energy, and the electric atmosphere of gameday.

Team Celebration

Wide-angle shots that capture the collective euphoria of a team moment. Show the full context — players, stadium, crowd energy all together.

Key guidelines: Use color to convey energy. Avoid tight crops for team shots — the sense of scale and collective is the point. Ensure stadium and crowd are part of the frame.

Photography Do's

Use B&W for dramatic/editorial moments. Lead with emotion and authenticity. Crop tightly for portraits, wide for team and fan moments. Show the diversity of the NFL fanbase and player body.

Photography Don'ts

Do not use over-filtered or heavily processed color images. Do not crop players in ways that look awkward or unflattering. Do not use images that feel staged or inauthentic. Do not use B&W for fan celebration or community imagery.
