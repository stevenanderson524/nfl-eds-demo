NFL Visual Identity

Photography Styles

The NFL photography style employs dynamic and static images to capture the essence of the game and the values of the NFL brand. When selecting photography, it is important to first determine the communication's desired impact and then choose images accordingly.

Dramatic Cropping

The NFL's photographic style relies on the creative cropping of images to generate drama in otherwise mundane photographs. Focusing on specific photographic areas without framing obvious subjects brings the excitement of the game to life and contributes to a uniquely NFL visual style.

Key technique: Tight crops isolate the moment. Black-and-white treatment removes distraction and forces focus on the subject's intensity and emotion.

Field Photography

While full-color action photography is frequently appropriate, still and detailed images of the field, stands, and sidelines are also recommended. These images capture the atmosphere and scale of the NFL experience beyond the action on the field.

Key technique: Wide-angle shots that capture the collective euphoria of a team moment. Show the full context — players, stadium, crowd energy all together.

Unidentified Players

In some cases it is necessary to mask player and team identities in NFL and related communications. This may be achieved by cropping photographs to partially obscure jerseys and by using motion filters.

Key technique: High contrast black-and-white processing. Leave breathing room at the crop edges — the subject should feel contained, not cramped. The athlete's emotion is the story, not the team affiliation.

Photography Do's

Use black-and-white for dramatic and editorial moments. Lead with emotion and authenticity. Crop tightly for portraits, wide for team and fan moments. Show the diversity of the NFL fanbase and player body.

Photography Don'ts

Do not use over-filtered or heavily processed color images. Do not crop players in ways that look awkward or unflattering. Do not use images that feel staged or inauthentic. Do not use black-and-white for fan celebration or community imagery.
