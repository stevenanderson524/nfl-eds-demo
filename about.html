Our Mission

The Game Itself

The NFL is committed to advancing the game of professional football in the United States and around the world. Our mission is to present the highest-quality professional football to fans at stadiums and through broadcast and digital platforms.

We are dedicated to player safety, officiating excellence, and a competitive product that reflects the very best the sport has to offer.

Beyond the Field

The NFL believes in the power of football to make a positive impact beyond the game. Through initiatives in education, health, and community engagement, the NFL and its clubs invest in the communities they serve.

NFL PLAY 60, the league's national youth health and fitness campaign, encourages children to be active for 60 minutes each day.

A Century of Football

1920s–1940s

The Founding Era

The American Professional Football Association was founded on August 20, 1920 in Canton, Ohio. Jim Thorpe, one of America's greatest athletes, was elected president.

In 1922, the organization was renamed the National Football League. Early powerhouses included the Canton Bulldogs, Chicago Bears, and Green Bay Packers.

The NFL held its first Championship Game in 1933, with the Chicago Bears defeating the New York Giants 23-21. The league would grow steadily through the 1940s despite World War II.

1950s–1970s

Growth and Rivalry

The 1958 NFL Championship Game — "The Greatest Game Ever Played" — between the Baltimore Colts and New York Giants helped establish professional football as a premier sport on television.

In 1960, the American Football League (AFL) was founded to compete with the NFL. The two leagues merged in 1970, creating the modern NFL with its two conferences: the AFC and NFC.

The Super Bowl was first played on January 15, 1967. The Green Bay Packers defeated the Kansas City Chiefs 35-10 in what was then called the AFL-NFL World Championship Game.

1980s–2000s

The Modern Era Begins

The 1980s and 90s produced legendary dynasties: the San Francisco 49ers won four Super Bowls in nine years with Joe Montana and Jerry Rice, while the Dallas Cowboys captured three titles in four years.

The 2001 New England Patriots, led by Bill Belichick and a young Tom Brady, began what would become the greatest dynasty in NFL history — winning six Super Bowls through 2019.

NFL Sunday Ticket launched in 1994, allowing fans to watch every out-of-market game. The NFL's digital presence expanded dramatically through the 2000s.

2010s–Today

The Next Generation

The 2010s brought new dynasties and new stars. Patrick Mahomes emerged as the face of the Kansas City Chiefs dynasty, capturing multiple Super Bowl titles and MVP awards.

The NFL expanded internationally, playing regular season games in London, Germany, and Brazil, signaling its ambitions as a truly global sport.

Today, the NFL features a new generation of dynamic quarterbacks — Lamar Jackson, Josh Allen, Jalen Hurts, and Mahomes — delivering record-breaking performances and thrilling fans every Sunday.

NFL Leadership

Commissioner

Roger Goodell

Roger Goodell has served as NFL Commissioner since 2006. Under his leadership, the NFL has grown to become the most valuable sports league in the world.

Key initiatives include expanding international games, the Las Vegas relocation of the Raiders, player safety improvements, and the establishment of NFL Media.

Governance

The NFL is structured as a membership organization of 32 clubs. The Commissioner is appointed by team owners and is responsible for managing league operations.

The NFL Competition Committee, comprised of team executives and coaches, recommends rule changes to improve the game and protect player safety each offseason.
